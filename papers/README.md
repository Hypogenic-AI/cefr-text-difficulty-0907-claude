# Papers Collection: CEFR Text Difficulty Research

This directory contains 18 papers related to modeling text difficulty using CEFR (Common European Framework of Reference for Languages) levels.

## Downloaded Papers

| # | Filename | Citation | Description |
|---|----------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | `arase2022_cefr_sp.pdf` | Arase, Y., Uchida, S., & Kajiwara, T. (2022). "CEFR-Based Sentence Difficulty Annotation and Assessment." *EMNLP 2022*. | Introduces the CEFR-SP dataset with sentence-level CEFR annotations and benchmarks models for sentence difficulty prediction. |
| 2 | `xia2016_readability_l2.pdf` | Xia, M., Kochmar, E., & Briscoe, T. (2016). "Text Readability Assessment for Second Language Learners." *BEA Workshop @ NAACL 2016*. | Investigates readability assessment specifically tailored for L2 English learners, comparing traditional and NLP-based features. |
| 3 | `vajjala2018_universal_cefr.pdf` | Vajjala, S., & Rama, T. (2018). "Experiments with Universal CEFR Classification." *BEA Workshop @ NAACL 2018*. | Explores cross-lingual and universal approaches to CEFR-level classification across multiple languages. |
| 4 | `khallaf2021_arabic_cefr.pdf` | Khallaf, N., & Sharoff, S. (2021). "Automatic Difficulty Classification of Arabic Sentences." *arXiv:2103.04386*. | Applies CEFR-based difficulty classification to Arabic text using linguistic features and machine learning. |
| 5 | `lagutina2023_cefr_ml_bert.pdf` | Lagutina, K., et al. (2023). "Text Complexity Classification Using Machine Learning and BERT." *MDPI journal*. | Compares traditional ML approaches with BERT-based models for classifying text complexity at CEFR levels. |
| 6 | `arnold2018_cefr_metrics.pdf` | Arnold, T., Ballier, N., Gaillat, T., & Lisson, P. (2018). "Predicting CEFR Levels in Learner English on the Basis of Metrics and Full Texts." *BEA Workshop*. | Evaluates the predictive power of various linguistic metrics versus full-text representations for CEFR level prediction in learner English. |
| 7 | `imperial2025_universalcefr.pdf` | Imperial, J. M., et al. (2025). "UniversalCEFR: Enabling Open Multilingual Research on Language Proficiency Assessment." *EMNLP 2025*. | Presents UniversalCEFR, a large-scale multilingual benchmark dataset and framework for CEFR-based proficiency assessment across many languages. |
| 8 | `pilan2016_readable_read.pdf` | Pilan, I., Vajjala, S., & Volodina, E. (2016). "A Readable Read: Automatic Assessment of Language Learning Materials based on Linguistic Complexity." *arXiv:1603.08868*. | Develops methods for automatically assessing the linguistic complexity of language learning materials using multi-level linguistic features. |
| 9 | `santucci2020_text_complexity.pdf` | Santucci, V., et al. (2020). "Automatic Classification of Text Complexity." *(Italian CEFR)*. | Addresses automatic CEFR-level classification for Italian texts, contributing methods for non-English language difficulty assessment. |
| 10 | `naous2023_readme_plus.pdf` | Naous, T., et al. (2023). "ReadMe++: Benchmarking Multilingual Language Models for Multi-Domain Readability Assessment." *arXiv*. | Introduces ReadMe++, a multilingual multi-domain benchmark for evaluating language models on readability assessment tasks. |
| 11 | `fujinuma2021_joint_readability.pdf` | Fujinuma, Y., & Hagiwara, M. (2021). "Graph-Based Joint Word-Document Readability Assessment." *Findings of EMNLP 2021*. | Proposes a graph-based approach that jointly models word and document readability for improved text difficulty estimation. |
| 12 | `malik2024_tarzan_tolkien.pdf` | Malik, A., et al. (2024). "From Tarzan to Tolkien: Controlling the Language Proficiency Level of LLMs." *ACL Findings 2024*. | Investigates methods for controlling the output language proficiency level of large language models to match target CEFR levels. |
| 13 | `francois2009_ffl_readability.pdf` | Francois, T., & Fairon, C. (2009/2012). "An AI Readability Formula for French as a Foreign Language." | Develops a readability formula specifically designed for French as a foreign language, incorporating CEFR-aligned difficulty features. |
| 14 | `jamet2024_french_difficulty.pdf` | Jamet, L., et al. (2024). "French Difficulty Assessment with LLMs." | Evaluates the use of large language models for assessing the difficulty level of French texts in a CEFR-aligned framework. |
| 15 | `kogan2025_ace_cefr.pdf` | Kogan, V., et al. (2025). "Ace-CEFR: Automated Evaluation of Linguistic Difficulty of Conversational Texts." *arXiv:2506.14046*. | Proposes Ace-CEFR, a system for automatically evaluating the CEFR-level linguistic difficulty of conversational and dialogue texts. |
| 16 | `imperial2023_flesch_fumble.pdf` | Imperial, J. M., et al. (2023). "Flesch or Fumble? Evaluating Readability Standard Alignment of Instruction-Tuned LLMs." | Examines whether instruction-tuned LLMs can reliably produce text at specified readability levels, finding significant alignment gaps. |
| 17 | `vajjala2012_sla_readability.pdf` | Vajjala, S., & Meurers, D. (2012). "On Improving the Accuracy of Readability Classification using Insights from Second Language Acquisition." *BEA Workshop @ NAACL 2012*. | Demonstrates that incorporating features motivated by second language acquisition research improves readability classification accuracy. |
| 18 | `pitler2008_readability.pdf` | Pitler, E., & Nenkova, A. (2008). "Revisiting Readability: A Unified Framework for Predicting Text Quality." *EMNLP 2008*. | Proposes a unified framework for readability prediction that integrates discourse, syntactic, and vocabulary features for predicting text quality. |
